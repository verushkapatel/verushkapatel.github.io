About

I am a fifteen-year-old independent researcher in Pune, India. My work sits at the intersection of financial economics, machine learning, and market microstructure — studying how textual disclosures, investor sentiment, and firm-level AI exposure shape systematic risk and the cross-section of stock returns.

My first paper, written in Grade 9, used machine learning to detect early-warning signals of stock market crashes and was named a semifinalist at the Columbia Junior Science Journal, where it was reviewed by Dr. Scott Romeika of the Wharton School. Since then I have completed three further working papers on disclosure quality, disclosure homogenization, and the long-run convergence of AI and ESG. I am currently conducting research under the mentorship of Jingshu Wen (PhD, Oxford Saïd Business School) on whether firm-level exposure to artificial intelligence is a priced systematic risk factor in the cross-section of stock returns.

I am actively looking for research collaboration, mentorship, and critical feedback from professors and graduate researchers. If anything here sparks an idea, please write.

Current research

Firm-level AI exposure as a priced systematic risk factor

In progress · Mentor: Jingshu Wen, PhD (Oxford Saïd Business School)

Investigating whether firm-level exposure to artificial intelligence is a priced systematic risk factor in the cross-section of stock returns. The project constructs firm-level AI-exposure measures from textual sources and tests whether they earn a significant risk premium after controlling for standard asset-pricing factors.

Papers & working papers

Disclosure Originality and Stock Volatility in the Age of AI

Working paper · 2,609 firm-years, S&P 500, 2018–2024

Introduces peer originality — the semantic distance between a firm’s 10-K risk disclosure and its sector peers, measured with sentence-transformer embeddings — as a novel, content-based measure of disclosure quality. Firms with more original disclosures exhibit significantly lower forward stock volatility (t = −3.50), with quasi-causal evidence from the SEC’s 2021 Regulation S-K Item 105 reform. An extension documents a structural break in “AI-washing” around the November 2022 release of ChatGPT, as boilerplate AI language saturated nearly half of S&P 500 filings.

Disclosure Homogenization and the Erosion of Diversification Benefits

Working paper · Evidence from 10-K embeddings, 2019–2024

Constructs a sector-level measure of disclosure homogenization (HomSim) using sentence-transformer embeddings of 10-K Item 1A risk factors across S&P 500 firms. Sector homogenization rose persistently from 0.40 in 2019 to 0.55 in 2024, and predicts significantly higher within-sector stock return correlations (t = 2.11, placebo p = 0.011). The paper argues that linguistic convergence in mandatory disclosures is converting idiosyncratic risk into systematic risk and compressing the feasible diversification set available to portfolio investors.

Beyond the Black Swan: Using Artificial Intelligence to Predict Stock Market Crashes

CJSJ Semifinalist · Reviewed by Dr. Scott Romeika, Wharton

Tests whether AI-driven models can detect early-warning signals of stock market crashes more effectively than traditional tools such as Value-at-Risk and CAPM. Combining market volatility, liquidity, trading volume, and NLP-based sentiment indicators from 2000 to 2023, Random Forest and Long Short-Term Memory models flagged systemic instability weeks before the Dot-com, 2008, and COVID-19 crashes, outperforming conventional methods and revealing that financial shocks leave identifiable digital footprints.

Redefining Global Prosperity: The Convergence of AI and ESG in the Economy of 2050

Submitted to the Journal of Student Research

Argues that the convergence of Artificial Intelligence and Environmental, Social, and Governance principles will redefine global prosperity by 2050, with AI functioning as the primary engine of efficiency and ESG as the anchor of resilience. Combines historical analysis of prior industrial revolutions, empirical trends in AI adoption and ESG investment, and scenario forecasting to project how productivity, labor markets, capital flows, and governance may be reshaped.
